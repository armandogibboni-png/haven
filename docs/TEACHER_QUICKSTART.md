# Haven — Teacher Quick-Start Guide

*Estimated reading time: 5 minutes. Estimated setup time: 10 minutes.*

---

## What is Haven?

Haven is a private app that helps you and the student's family work together on their SEN / SM plan — without needing a formal meeting for every small update.

Your access is **strictly limited**:
- ✅ You can view and contribute to the student's SEN Plan
- ✅ You can message the student's parents
- ✅ You can post on the Family Board
- ❌ You cannot see the student's personal chats, emotion check-ins, or victories diary
- ❌ You cannot see any child-generated data

Your profile is **PIN-protected**. Keep your PIN private.

---

## Step 1 — Open Haven

Go to the URL shared with you (e.g. `haven-yourschool.vercel.app`) on your phone or computer. No app download needed.

Enter your name, choose the **Teacher / School** role, pick your emoji, and set a 4-digit PIN. You will need this PIN every time you access your profile.

---

## Step 2 — Open a student's SEN Plan

From your teacher home, tap the student's name. You will see the SEN Plan with 4 tabs:

| Tab | What it's for |
|-----|--------------|
| 🎯 Goals | SMART goals by area. Add GAS levels (optional) to define 5 outcome levels at review. |
| 📝 Feedback | Log brief observations. Takes ~2 minutes. |
| 🔄 Revisions | Propose a plan change. Parents approve or reject. |
| ✨ Summary | AI synthesis of recent observations. 3/6-month check-ins. |

---

## Step 3 — Log your first observation

Tap **📝 Feedback → + Add observation**. Write 1–3 sentences about what you observed this week. Add the context if helpful (e.g. "small group, quiet room").

**Minimum commitment: 1 observation per student per week.** The fidelity badge (🟢/🟡/🔴) shows you whether you're on track.

---

## Step 4 — Using the AI in your teacher area

Your AI companion can help with:
- Classroom strategies for SM and SEN students
- How to frame conversations with parents
- Interpreting SEN plan progress
- Preparing for review meetings

It **cannot** tell you about the student's personal data, and all advice is **educational support, not clinical guidance**.

---

## The crisis button 🆘

If you are concerned about a student's wellbeing, tap the **🆘 red button** in the top-right of the SEN Plan. This will show you:
- NCSE SENO contacts (Ireland)
- HSE CAMHS referral pathway
- NEPS (educational psychology)
- Tusla (child and family agency)

Haven is not a clinical tool. If you have serious concerns, contact these services directly and follow your school's safeguarding procedures.

---

## GDPR — what you need to know

- All student data you access in Haven is data the school already holds (IEP / SEN plan)
- You cannot access any child-generated data
- The school's data protection policy applies
- Parental consent was obtained before you were enrolled
- Questions? Contact the Haven project lead.

---

## Quick reference

| Action | Where |
|--------|-------|
| Log an observation | SEN Plan → 📝 Feedback → + Add observation |
| Add a goal | SEN Plan → 🎯 Goals → + Add Goal |
| Propose a revision | SEN Plan → 🔄 Revisions → Propose revision |
| Message a parent | Teacher home → Contact parents |
| Crisis / concern | SEN Plan → 🆘 button (top right) |
| Export plan | SEN Plan → ↓ Export (top right) |
